Bootcamp Learning Plan

Devacademy has already challenged me in ways I didn't expect to be challenged, and I can only imagine I will be challenged even more as the weeks go by. I can already tell a few things from the past few weeks of foundations:

  • Getting back into the study mindset can be a struggle. Your brain takes up a lot of energy to change neural pathways, and that energy doesn't come from nowhere. The more effort you put in to shifting that mindset early on, the more rewards you will reap later on.
  • I need to (and have been) challenge myself to speak up when I need more information or help. Devacademy is already very strong on communicating this idea, but it bears repeating. Reach. Out.
  • It's important to book in your rest time. You can't function without sleep and relaxation, so carve out times for those things and don't compromise!

In my foundations learning plan I mentioned that a weakness of mine is that I am heavily affected by my environment, and that I need to shape that environment in order to produce the best results. In reflection I think this holds true. It's a truth about myself that I have learned over years and years of winding round this mortal coil. There is one thing I would add to that, and that is: knowledge is not enough. It's important to put aside time to do the work of shaping the environment, otherwise it's of no use. This will be especially important as I face the increased time-management demands of Bootcamp.

That being said, foundations faculty have been amazing so far, and if I would ask for anything it's: more of that! The reaching out, touching base, feedback, everything, has been stellar. Everyone seems to be genuinely passionate about what they're doing, and it makes me want to learn even more.
